Start the Applied Practice Course

You must be enrolled in — or have started — the BCFSA Applied Practice Course (APC), which includes brokerage-supervised assignments.

3

Common questions

FAQ

If you're a first-time licensee completing APC brokerage-supervised assignments, yes — a Mentor Agreement must be in place before you start supervised work. Experienced licensees transferring from another brokerage typically do not require a mentor.

During onboarding, you can request a specific mentor from the roster or ask to be matched. Browse the available individual mentors and teams through the links in the Resources section above.

A team typically offers broader support — multiple agents, shared resources, and team-level systems. An individual mentor provides one-on-one guidance. Both satisfy the mentorship requirement; choose what fits your learning style.

When your APC supervised requirements are satisfied and onboarding confirms completion. There's no fixed timeline — it depends on how quickly you work through your assignments.

Check the mentorship program details on Real Support for current eligibility requirements for mentors. Generally, you need an active licence with sufficient experience and good standing.

Start with your mentor. For onboarding or agreement issues, contact the Real Broker onboarding team directly. For regulatory questions, refer to BCFSA.
