Eligibility requirements

To join Real Broker BC as a brand new licensee, you must meet these minimum requirements.

1) BCFSA first-time license

You must have a First Time License issued by BCFSA.

2) Applied Practice Course started

You must have completed a portion of the Applied Practice Course (APC).

3) Mentor Agreement in place

Mentor Agreement must be completed through onboarding before supervised assignments.

How the program works

Mentorship gives you supervised reps while you complete APC Brokerage-supervised assignments.

Mentor responsibilities

  • Supervise and guide APC supervised assignments
  • Provide transaction support + practical guidance
  • Reinforce compliance and best practices

Agent responsibilities

  • Complete APC requirements on schedule
  • Use mentor guidance for deals + compliance questions
  • Follow Real Broker and BCFSA standards

FAQ

Short, direct answers.

Do I need a mentor to join Real Broker BC as a new licensee?

If you are a first-time licensee completing the Applied Practice Course with Brokerage-supervised assignments, yes — you need a Mentor Agreement completed through onboarding.

Who completes the Mentor Agreement?

Onboarding coordinates the paperwork. You and the Mentor align on expectations and supervision requirements.

When does mentorship end?

When the APC supervised requirements are satisfied and onboarding confirms completion.

Where do I start?

Confirm eligibility → connect with onboarding → complete Mentor Agreement → complete APC supervised requirements.

Who do I contact if I’m stuck?

Start with your Mentor. For onboarding/agreement issues, contact onboarding.
